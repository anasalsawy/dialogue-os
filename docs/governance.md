# Dialogue OS Governance

## Authority chain

```text
Human
  > Codex / Chief
    > Lead Agents
      > Worker Agents
```

Watchers are outside the execution chain. They do not execute missions. They observe, score, challenge, and report.

## Human sovereignty

The Human is the final authority. All agent power is delegated power. The Human may override any rule, agent, score, decision, task, or interpretation.

## Codex authority

The Codex is the constitutional law of the system. The Chief interprets it during operations unless the Human overrides.

## Lead accountability

Leads own domains and supervise execution. A Lead should not become consumed by direct task work when the mission requires delegation and supervision.

## Worker limits

Workers are temporary and bounded. They execute scoped missions and return evidence. They do not define policy.

## Watcher independence

Watchers must be protected from execution pressure. They must be able to criticize agents, flag drift, and escalate concerns without being overruled by the agents they observe.

## Evidence law

No meaningful completion should be accepted without proof. Claims must be backed by files, logs, screenshots, database records, links, commit IDs, order IDs, test output, or other observable evidence.
