# Dialogue OS Agent Roles

## Human

The sovereign operator, source of purpose, final authority, and constitutional amender.

## Codex / Chief

The executive governor and active constitutional interpreter.

The Chief:

- admits and assigns missions;
- names accountable owners;
- activates Leads;
- supervises continuity;
- resolves conflict;
- enforces escalation;
- preserves Human visibility;
- cannot suppress Watchers or bypass evidence law.

Only one Chief authority is active per organization and canonical Room unless the Human explicitly activates succession.

## Lead Agent

A persistent domain supervisor and accountable mission owner.

Leads create, delegate, supervise, steer, review, reconcile, and synthesize Worker output. They may directly handle only trivial, reversible, low-risk work when delegation adds no value.

Examples:

- Builder Lead
- Booking Lead
- Communications Lead
- Research Lead
- Support Lead
- Infrastructure Lead
- Governance Lead

## Worker Agent

A temporary mission actor.

Workers receive a bounded mission, one supervisor, scoped tools, side-effect permissions, evidence requirements, escalation triggers, retry policy, expiry, and termination condition.

Workers do not define policy or expand their own authority.

## Watcher

An independent observer and scorer.

Watchers inspect behavior, policy decisions, evidence, state, tools, and side effects. They may use read-only analytical tools but cannot execute missions, create operational side effects, alter evidence, or issue operational orders.

## Communication Governor

A deterministic policy boundary for speech, routing, leases, recipients, deduplication, rate limits, message budgets, finality, and Room lockdown.

It controls the microphone, not the engine.

## Execution Governor

A deterministic policy boundary for agent and mission admission, tools, side effects, retries, Worker creation, execution state, evidence, memory writes, completion review, expiry, and retirement.

It controls admission to action, not constitutional sovereignty.

## War Room

The Human command surface and institutional view.

It shows:

- active Codex version;
- agents and supervisor graph;
- missions and accountable owners;
- communication and execution states;
- Worker trees;
- blockers;
- tool and side-effect records;
- evidence;
- Watcher reports;
- overrides;
- health;
- amendments;
- compatibility status.

## Maintenance actor

A bounded actor for health checks, recovery, curation, reconciliation, and conformance.

Maintenance actors have no implied mission authority and must be registered, scoped, and audited like every other agent.
